Tomato - Bacterial Spot

Symptoms:

  • Small, water-soaked spots on leaves and fruits.
  • Yellow halos around the spots.
  • Lesions that coalesce to form larger necrotic areas.

Cure:

  • Use disease-free seeds.
  • Apply copper-based fungicides.
  • Rotate crops to reduce bacterial build-up.

Tomato - Early Blight

Symptoms:

  • Dark, concentric spots on lower leaves.
  • Yellowing of leaves around the lesions.
  • Premature leaf drop.

Cure:

  • Prune affected leaves.
  • Apply fungicides containing chlorothalonil.
  • Practice crop rotation and mulching.

Tomato - Late Blight

Symptoms:

  • Dark, water-soaked spots on leaves and stems.
  • Fruits develop brown, firm patches.
  • White fungal growth under leaves in humid conditions.

Cure:

  • Destroy infected plants immediately.
  • Apply fungicides containing mancozeb or chlorothalonil.
  • Use resistant tomato varieties.

Tomato - Leaf Mold

Symptoms:

  • Yellowish-green spots on upper leaf surfaces.
  • Grayish-purple mold on the undersides of leaves.
  • Leaves may curl and die.

Cure:

  • Improve air circulation around plants.
  • Apply fungicides containing chlorothalonil.
  • Remove and destroy infected plant debris.

Tomato - Septoria Leaf Spot

Symptoms:

  • Small, circular spots with gray centers and dark edges.
  • Yellowing and browning of leaves.
  • Premature defoliation.

Cure:

  • Remove infected leaves and debris.
  • Apply fungicides containing copper or mancozeb.
  • Avoid overhead watering.

Tomato - Spider Mites

Symptoms:

  • Tiny yellow or white speckles on leaves.
  • Fine webbing on the undersides of leaves.
  • Leaves may turn brown and fall off.

Cure:

  • Spray plants with water to dislodge mites.
  • Apply insecticidal soap or neem oil.
  • Introduce natural predators like ladybugs.

Tomato - Target Spot

Symptoms:

  • Dark, concentric rings on leaves and fruits.
  • Lesions may have a "bullseye" appearance.
  • Premature leaf drop.

Cure:

  • Remove and destroy infected plant parts.
  • Apply fungicides containing chlorothalonil.
  • Practice crop rotation.

Tomato - Yellow Leaf Curl Virus

Symptoms:

  • Yellowing and upward curling of leaves.
  • Stunted plant growth.
  • Reduced fruit production.

Cure:

  • Use resistant tomato varieties.
  • Control whitefly populations (the virus vector).
  • Remove and destroy infected plants.

Tomato - Mosaic Virus

Symptoms:

  • Mottled light and dark green patches on leaves.
  • Leaf distortion and stunted growth.
  • Reduced fruit yield and quality.

Cure:

  • Use virus-free seeds and transplants.
  • Disinfect tools and hands after handling infected plants.
  • Remove and destroy infected plants.
